Dreaming

HOME • TWITTER • 01/08/18

So life has kind of happened in the past few weeks.

I'd like to announce that I have a new gig. I'm to be an assistant educator at Coder Academy facilitating the next bootcamp which starts on the 20th of August and runs for a tad over 20 weeks. The role is ongoing and has the opportunity to progress into other bootcamp variations which Coder Academy run (such as this one).

This is a big moment in my life. A moment of vindication. A post I wrote earlier this year was all about why I'm taking a risk and committing full time to learning how to code. Reading that back after hearing the good news about this new job; I almost shed a tear.

I trusted my gut back then. I knew I enjoyed coding. I just didn't know where it would lead me and how hard it would be be. I decided to enroll at the Le Wagon bootcamp based on this gut feeling alone. It was fucking scary actually having to front up with the cash rather than relying on HEX. I think now (with hindsight) it was absolutely fucking worth it.

The first few weeks were incredibly stressful. Learning Ruby (or any programming language) is a grind especially when you're doing it every day and compressing content down to a bootcamp format.

In saying this, it's a great way to learn. You're forced to be uncomfortable and that's life. Work is uncomfortable and success is uncomfortable. Your brain will hurt a lot of the time but I'm telling you that it's worth it.

I now have a family and a network within the Melbourne web development community. The meet ups and events that are run for free are awesome for making new friends and improving as a developer. It's an amazing community. I don't believe that there's many other industries with the type of community that developers in Melbourne have.

When I walked out of the interview I was kind of telling myself "what just happened". I was shaking from joy. I could have high fived a random person in the street. This is my life now. I have a job that I'm really proud of. A role that I'm really really excited to start.

So that's about it. Now the real work begins.
